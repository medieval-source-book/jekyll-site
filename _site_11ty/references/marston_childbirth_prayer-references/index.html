Jones, Peter Murray, and Lea T Olsan. “Performative Rituals for Conception and Childbirth in England, 900-1500.”

Bulletin of the History of Medicine , vol. 89,3 (2015): 406-33.

This paper focuses on the performative aspect of childbirth prayers and explores the uses of chanting to aid childbirth.

Olsan, Lea. “Latin Charms of Medieval England: Verbal Healing in a Christian Oral Tradition.” Oral Tradition , 7/1 (1992):116-142.

This paper provides a detailed analysis of childbirth charms and prayers and a discussion of the differences between them.

Wood, Diana. Women and Religion in Medieval England . Oxford: Oxbow Books, 2003.

This book is a general overview of the religious life of medieval English women. There is a specific chapter on women and child birth on pages 91-117.
